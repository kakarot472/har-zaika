Dum Aloo

Prep: 15 min
Cook: 35 min
Serves: 4-5

Ingredients

  • 500g small potatoes, boiled and peeled
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 3-4 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 cup yogurt, whisked
  • 1/2 cup cashews, soaked in warm water
  • 3-4 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 1 inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1/2 tsp fennel powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Boil potatoes until tender, peel and prick with a fork. Set aside.
  2. Grind soaked cashews with a little water to make a smooth paste.
  3. Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add cumin seeds, bay leaves, cardamom, and cinnamon.
  4. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown (8-10 minutes).
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for 2 minutes.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they break down and become pulpy.
  7. Add red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, and fennel powder. Mix well.
  8. Add whisked yogurt gradually, stirring continuously to prevent curdling.
  9. Add cashew paste and mix well. Cook for 3-4 minutes.
  10. Add boiled potatoes and gently mix to coat with the gravy.
  11. Add salt and 1/2 cup water. Bring to a boil.
  12. Cover and cook on low heat (dum) for 15-20 minutes.
  13. Sprinkle garam masala and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  14. Serve hot with rice, naan, or roti.

Chef's Tips

  • Use small, uniform-sized potatoes for even cooking
  • Prick boiled potatoes with a fork to help absorb flavors
  • Add yogurt gradually while stirring to prevent curdling
  • Cashew paste gives the gravy a rich, creamy texture
  • Cook on low heat (dum) for authentic flavor development
  • Adjust spice levels according to your preference
