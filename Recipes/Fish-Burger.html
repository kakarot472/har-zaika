Fish Burger

Prep: 15 min
Cook: 20 min
Serves: 2

Ingredients

For Fish Fillet:

  • 2 white fish fillets (cod or tilapia)
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

For Tartar Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp pickle relish
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Assembly:


  • 2 burger buns
  • 2 lettuce leaves
  • 2 tomato slices
  • 2 cheese slices (optional)
  • Red onion slices (optional)

Instructions

  1. Pat fish fillets dry and season with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and paprika.
  2. Set up breading station: flour in one dish, beaten egg in another, breadcrumbs in third.
  3. Dredge fish in flour, then egg, then breadcrumbs, pressing gently to adhere.
  4. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  5. Fry fish fillets for 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through.
  6. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  7. For tartar sauce, mix mayonnaise, pickle relish, lemon juice, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  8. Toast burger buns lightly until golden.
  9. Spread tartar sauce on both halves of each bun.
  10. Place lettuce leaf on bottom bun, add fried fish fillet.
  11. Add cheese slice (if using), tomato slice, and red onion.
  12. Top with other half of bun and serve immediately.

Chef's Tips

  • Use fresh, firm white fish for best results
  • Don't overcook fish - it should flake easily when done
  • Press breadcrumbs gently to ensure good coating
  • Oil temperature should be medium-high to avoid soggy coating
  • Toast buns to prevent sogginess from sauce
  • Serve immediately while fish is hot and crispy
