Sindhi Biryani

Prep: 30 min
Cook: 45 min
Serves: 6-8

Ingredients

For Rice:

  • 3 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 2 black cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt to taste

For Chicken:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

For Biryani:

  • 3 large onions, thinly sliced and fried
  • 3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 potatoes, cubed and fried
  • 1/2 cup plums (aloo bukhara), soaked
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup almonds, sliced
  • 1/2 cup mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup coriander leaves
  • 1/2 cup ghee
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1/2 tsp saffron soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk
  • 2 tbsp biryani masala powder

Instructions

  1. Marinate chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, and salt for 30 minutes.
  2. Boil water in a large pot with whole spices and salt. Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat ghee and cook marinated chicken until tender. Remove and set aside.
  4. In the same pot, add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft. Add biryani masala and garam masala.
  5. Layer the cooked chicken at the bottom of the pot.
  6. Add fried potatoes, plums, and raisins over the chicken.
  7. Spread half the rice over the chicken layer.
  8. Sprinkle half the fried onions, mint, coriander, and almonds.
  9. Add remaining rice as the top layer.
  10. Top with remaining fried onions, herbs, almonds, and saffron milk.
  11. Cover with aluminum foil, then place the lid. Cook on high heat for 3-4 minutes.
  12. Reduce heat to low and cook for 45 minutes (dum cooking).
  13. Let it rest for 10 minutes before opening. Gently mix and serve hot.

Chef's Tips

  • Soak basmati rice for at least 30 minutes for longer grains
  • Fry onions until deep golden brown for rich color and flavor
  • Don't skip the dum cooking process - it's essential for authentic biryani
  • Plums (aloo bukhara) give Sindhi biryani its distinctive sweet-tangy flavor
  • Use saffron soaked in warm milk for beautiful color and aroma
  • Let the biryani rest after cooking for flavors to meld together
