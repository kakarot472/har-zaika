Keema Bharay Karelay

Prep: 30 min
Cook: 45 min
Serves: 4-5

Ingredients

For Bitter Gourds:


  • 8-10 small bitter gourds (karela)
  • 2 tbsp salt (for debittering)
  • 1 tbsp turmeric powder

For Keema Filling:

  • 500g ground beef or mutton
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 3-4 tbsp cooking oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped

For Gravy:

  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup yogurt, whisked
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions

  1. Wash bitter gourds and scrape the outer skin lightly. Make a slit lengthwise and remove seeds.
  2. Rub with salt and turmeric, let sit for 30 minutes to remove bitterness. Rinse and pat dry.
  3. Heat oil in a pan and fry bitter gourds until lightly golden. Remove and set aside.
  4. In the same oil, add chopped onions and cook until golden brown.
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for 2 minutes.
  6. Add ground meat and cook on high heat, breaking up lumps, until browned.
  7. Add red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  8. Cook until meat is fully cooked and dry (15-20 minutes). Add garam masala and coriander leaves.
  9. Stuff the fried bitter gourds with the cooked keema mixture.
  10. Secure with toothpicks if needed.
  11. In the same pan, add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  12. Add whisked yogurt gradually, stirring continuously.
  13. Add stuffed bitter gourds to the gravy. Add water and garam masala.
  14. Cover and simmer for 15-20 minutes until tender.
  15. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with rice or roti.

Chef's Tips

  • Salt and turmeric treatment is essential to reduce bitterness
  • Choose small, tender bitter gourds for easier stuffing
  • Cook keema until completely dry before stuffing
  • Add yogurt gradually to prevent curdling
  • Simmer gently to prevent bitter gourds from breaking
  • This dish tastes better the next day as flavors develop
