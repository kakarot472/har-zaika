Mutton Nehari

Prep: 30 min
Cook: 3 hours
Serves: 6-8

Ingredients

For Mutton:


  • 1 kg mutton with bones (shank pieces preferred)
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

For Nehari Masala:

  • 6-8 dried red chilies
  • 2 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tbsp cumin seeds
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 2 black cardamom pods
  • 1 inch cinnamon stick
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1/2 nutmeg
  • 2-3 mace blades

For Cooking:

  • 3-4 tbsp ghee
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced and fried
  • 3 tbsp wheat flour
  • Fresh ginger julienne for garnish
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Green chilies, slit for garnish
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. Dry roast all nehari masala ingredients until fragrant. Cool and grind to fine powder. Set aside.
  2. Marinate mutton with ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, and salt for 30 minutes.
  3. In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat ghee and add marinated mutton. Cook on high heat for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Add enough water to cover the meat completely. Bring to boil.
  5. Add 2 tbsp of prepared nehari masala powder. Cover and cook on low heat for 2-2.5 hours until meat is tender.
  6. In a small pan, dry roast wheat flour until golden. Mix with some cooking liquid to make smooth paste.
  7. Add the flour paste to the pot and mix well. This will thicken the gravy.
  8. Add fried onions and remaining nehari masala. Cook for 15-20 minutes more.
  9. Adjust salt and spices. The gravy should be thick and rich.
  10. Garnish with fresh ginger julienne, coriander leaves, and green chilies.
  11. Serve hot with naan, kulcha, or tandoori roti and lemon wedges.

Chef's Tips

  • Use mutton with bones (shank pieces) for authentic flavor and richness
  • Slow cooking for 2-3 hours is essential for tender meat and rich gravy
  • Freshly ground nehari masala makes a huge difference in taste
  • Wheat flour helps thicken the gravy - add gradually to avoid lumps
  • Traditionally served for breakfast, but perfect for any meal
  • The longer it cooks, the better the flavors develop
