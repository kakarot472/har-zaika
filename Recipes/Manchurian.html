Manchurian

Prep: 20 min
Cook: 25 min
Serves: 4-5

Ingredients

For Vegetable Balls:

  • 1 cup cabbage, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup carrot, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup capsicum, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup spring onions, chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

For Manchurian Sauce:

  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1/4 cup spring onions, chopped
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp tomato ketchup
  • 1 tbsp chili sauce
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 1/4 cup water
  • 1 cup water or vegetable stock
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Mix all chopped vegetables in a bowl. Add ginger-garlic paste, flour, cornstarch, soy sauce, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add water gradually to form a thick batter that binds the vegetables together.
  3. Heat oil for deep frying. Shape the mixture into small balls and deep fry until golden brown and crispy.
  4. Remove and drain on paper towels. Set aside.
  5. For the sauce, heat oil in a pan. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Saut√© for 1 minute.
  6. Add spring onions and cook for 30 seconds.
  7. Add soy sauce, tomato ketchup, chili sauce, vinegar, and sugar. Mix well.
  8. Add water or stock and bring to a boil.
  9. Add cornstarch slurry and stir continuously until sauce thickens.
  10. Add the fried vegetable balls to the sauce and toss gently to coat.
  11. Cook for 2-3 minutes until balls are well coated with sauce.
  12. Garnish with chopped spring onions and serve hot.

Chef's Tips

  • Squeeze excess water from vegetables before making balls
  • Fry the balls on medium heat to ensure they cook through
  • Don't add balls to sauce too early or they'll become soggy
  • Adjust spice levels by varying chili sauce quantity
  • Serve immediately for best texture and taste
  • Can be made with chicken instead of vegetables for non-vegetarian version
